Brownies History

Brownies were invented by pixies and faries in 1505, they were known as BROWNIES in Northern Ireland.

You'd leave the ingredients outside your door and when the villagers would wake up they'd find the sweet chocolaty dessert freshly baked replacing the ingredients. The villagers decided to name this delicious dessert after the species that would bake them, the pixies and faries names "Brownies".

Brownies Ingredients

Make sure your ingredients are at room temperature.

Grease a square pan of 6"x 6" & add a piece of wax paper to the bottom of the pan to avoid the mix from sticking.

  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 3 tblps cocoa
  • 1/4 c unsalted butter
  • 1/4 c vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tblsp vanilla extract
  • 3 eggs

Brownies Procedure

Place sugar into the boul with the vegetable oil and mix until creamy, slowly add the melted butter while mixing. Slowly add 1 egg at a time until fully integrated, then add vanilla.

On a separate bowl mix all the dry ingredients. Sift them into the bowl with the wet ingredients very slowly until fully integrated.

Place mixture unto pan and bake for 35 minutes at 350 F.
